Diabetes Dataset Codebook 

A data frame with 79954 observations on the following 22 variables:

diabetes_binary - A factor with levels: no diabetes and diabetes indicating a respondent's diabetes diagnosis

high_bp - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating a respondent does not have high blood pressure and 1 indicating they do high blood pressure 

high_chol - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating a respondent does not have high cholosterol and 1 indicating they do high cholosterol

chol_check - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating a respondent has not had a cholosterol check in five years and 1 indicating they have had a cholosterol check in five years 

smoker - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating a respondent has not smoked at least 100 cigarettes in their entire life and 1 indicating that they have

stroke - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating a respondent has not a stroke before and 1 indicating that they have

heart_diseaseor_attack - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating a respondent does not have coronary heart disease or myocardial infarction and 1 indicating that they have one of the two diseases

phys_activity - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating that a respondent has done physical activity in the past 30 days and 1 indicating that they have 

fruits - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating that a respondent does not consume fruit 1 or more
times per day and 1 indicating that they do consume fruit 1 or more times per day

veggies - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating that a respondent does not consume vegetables 1 or more times per day and 1 indicating that they do consume vegetables 1 or more times per day 

hvy_alcohol_consump - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating that a respondent is not a heavy drinker and 1 indicating that they are a heavy drinker 

any_healthcare - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating that a respondent does not have any kind of health care coverage and 1 indicating that they do have a form of health care coverage 

no_docbc_cost - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating that a respondent did not need to see a doctor in the past 12 months but could not because of cost and 1 indicating that they did have this experience

gen_hlth - A factor withs levels: good, very good, fair, excellent, and poor indicating how a respondent assessed their general health 

diff_walk - A factor with levels 0 and 1, with 0 indicating that a respondent does not have serious difficulty walking or climbing stairs and 1 indicating that they do have difficulty walking or climbing stairs

sex - A factor with levels: female and male to indicate a respondent's sex

bmi - Respondent's body mass index 

ment_hlth - Now thinking about respondent's mental health, which includes stress, depression, and problems with emotions, for how many days during the past 30 days was their mental health not good? scale 1-30 days

phys_hlth - Now thinking about respondent's physical health, which includes physical illness and injury, for how many days during the past 30 days was respondent's physical health not good? scale 1-30 days

age - 13-level age category, 1 = 18-24, 2 = 25-29, 3 = 30-34, 4 = 35-39, 5 = 40-44, 6 = 45-49, 7 = 50-54. 
8 = 55-59. 9 = 60-64, 10 = 65-69, 11 = 70-74, 12 = 75-79, 13 = 80 or older

education - Education level of respondent, scale 1-6, 1 = Never attended school or only kindergarten, 2 = Grades 1 through 8 (Elementary), 3 = Grades 9 through 11 (Some high school), 4 = Grade 12 or GED (High school graduate), 5 = College 1 year to 3 years (Some college or technical school), 6 = College 4 years or more (College graduate)	

income - Income scale, scale 1-8, 1 = less than $10,000, 2 = less than $15,000, 3 = less than $20,000,
4 = less than $25,000, 5 = less than $35,000, 6 = less than $50,000, 7 = less than $75,000, 
8 = $75,000 or more
